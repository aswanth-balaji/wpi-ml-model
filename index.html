4 Interaction Terms Implemented
① VM × Moisture max penalty: −6%
Low volatile matter + wet feed = flame extinction risk. Neither kills combustion alone at moderate levels; together they quench the raceway. Source: heat balance + Murai 2002 combustion photography.
② Rate × Particle Size max penalty: −5%
Only fires above 40 kg/tHM. High rate + large particles = char accumulation compounds. Permeability degrades faster than either factor alone predicts. Source: Asanuma 1997 deadman analysis.
③ O₂ × Blast Temp max penalty: −4%
O₂ benefit saturates at high blast temperature. When raceway already oxygen-rich from high temp, extra enrichment gives diminishing returns. Produces the 3.5% saturation curve. Source: ThyssenKrupp operational data.
④ VM × Injection Rate max penalty: −4%
Low VM plastic cannot sustain high injection rates — volatile release insufficient to maintain combustion front at high feed rates. Gates the safe operating injection rate by feedstock quality.

Honest Limitations
Training data: Synthetic — physics equations, not real DCS logs. Interaction term magnitudes calibrated to literature ranges, not plant measurements.
Interaction magnitudes: The -0.06 coefficient on VM×moisture is estimated from heat balance, not measured. Real magnitude needs validation against actual CE sensors.
Missing physics: No zinc cycling model, no slag viscosity effect, no tuyere wear degradation over campaign life.
Deployment gap: Real DCS integration requires ISA-88 validation, HAZOP review, and shadow-mode operation before any closed-loop control.
